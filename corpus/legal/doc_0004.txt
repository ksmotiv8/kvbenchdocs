**IV. FACTUAL BACKGROUND**  

A. **The Product in Question**  
The product at the center of this litigation is a line of biodegradable and eco-friendly cleaning agents sold under the brand name “EcoClean Pro.” These products were marketed and sold by Defendant as environmentally safe, non-toxic, and suitable for use in households, including kitchens and bathrooms. The product in question, specifically, was a concentrated version of the “EcoClean Pro” line, which was labeled as a “multi-surface cleaner” and was distributed in 16-ounce plastic bottles with instructions for dilution and use.  

B. **The Incident**  
On or about April 12, 2023, the Plaintiff, Sarah Mitchell, purchased a 16-ounce bottle of the “EcoClean Pro” multi-surface cleaner from a retail store located at 456 Main Street, Los Angeles, California. The bottle was unmarked except for the product label, which stated that the product was “non-toxic,” “eco-friendly,” and “safe for use on all surfaces.”  

On the same day, the Plaintiff used the product to clean a kitchen counter. After applying the cleaner to the surface, she left the area for approximately 15 minutes to attend to other tasks. Upon returning, she noticed a strong, pungent odor emanating from the surface. The surface appeared to be sticky and glistening, and upon closer inspection, the Plaintiff discovered that the cleaner had not been properly diluted, and instead had been applied undiluted.  

The Plaintiff attempted to clean the surface with a damp cloth and water, but the residue persisted, and it began to emit a strong, acrid smell that caused her to experience immediate irritation to her eyes, nose, and throat. She was unable to continue her normal activities and had to seek immediate medical attention at the Los Angeles Community Health Center.  


C. **Injuries Sustained**  
As a result of the exposure to the undiluted product, the Plaintiff suffered from a variety of injuries, including, but not limited to:  

1. **Respiratory Irritation:** The Plaintiff experienced severe coughing, difficulty breathing, and persistent wheezing.  
2. **Ocular Irritation:** The Plaintiff’s eyes became red, swollen, and extremely sensitive to light.  
3. **Dermal Irritation:** The Plaintiff suffered from a rash and irritation on her hands and arms, which persisted for several weeks.  
4. **Psychological Distress:** The Plaintiff experienced anxiety, panic attacks, and persistent fear of using similar products in the future.  
5. **Medical Expenses:** The Plaintiff incurred significant medical expenses, including diagnostic tests, treatment, and medications, as well as lost wages due to her inability to work during the recovery period.  

D. **Defective Nature of the Product**  
The Plaintiff’s injuries were directly caused by the defective nature of the product, which was not properly labeled, was not properly diluted, and was not safe for use as advertised. The product’s label did not contain any clear warnings or instructions regarding the necessity of dilution, and the packaging was misleading in its representation of the product’s safety and non-toxic nature.  

Furthermore, the Defendant’s marketing materials and promotional statements downplayed the potential hazards of using the product without proper dilution, which was a critical factor in the Plaintiff’s decision to use the product in its undiluted form.  

**V. NEGLIGENCE OF THE DEFENDANT**  


A. **Duty of Care**  
The Defendant, as a manufacturer and seller of consumer products, owed a duty of care to the Plaintiff and to all consumers who may use its products. This duty included, but was not limited to, the obligation to design, manufacture, and label products in a manner that is safe for their intended use and to warn consumers of potential risks and dangers associated with the product.  

B. **Breach of Duty**  
The Defendant breached its duty of care by:  

1. **Failing to Provide Adequate Warnings:** The product’s label failed to provide clear and conspicuous warnings regarding the necessity of dilution and the risks associated with using the product in its undiluted form.  
2. **Failing to Provide Proper Instructions:** The product’s instructions were incomplete and misleading, providing no clear guidance on how to dilute the product or how to use it safely.  
3. **Failing to Conduct Appropriate Testing:** The Defendant did not conduct adequate testing to determine the safety of the product in its undiluted form, and this failure was directly responsible for the Plaintiff’s injuries.  
4. **Misrepresenting Product Safety:** The Defendant misrepresented the product’s safety by promoting it as non-toxic and safe for use without dilution, which was not supported by the actual properties of the product.  

C. **Causation**  
The Defendant’s breach of its duty of care was a proximate cause of the Plaintiff’s injuries. The failure to warn and the failure to provide adequate instructions directly caused the Plaintiff to use the product in an unsafe manner, which in turn led to her exposure to the undiluted product and the subsequent injuries.  

D. **Damages**  
As a result of the Defendant’s negligence, the Plaintiff has suffered both economic and non-economic damages, including:  

1. **Medical Expenses:** The Plaintiff incurred significant medical expenses, including diagnostic testing, treatment, and medications.  
2. **Lost Wages:** The Plaintiff was unable to work for several weeks following the incident, resulting in a substantial loss of income.  
3. **Pain and Suffering:** The Plaintiff continues to suffer from physical and emotional pain, including chronic respiratory issues and anxiety related to the incident.  
4. **Loss of Enjoyment of Life:** The incident has significantly impacted the Plaintiff’s ability to engage in normal activities and has caused her to develop a lasting fear of using similar products in the future.  

**VI. STRICT LIABILITY IN PRODUCT LIABILITY**  

A. **Legal Standard**  
Under California law, a manufacturer, seller, or distributor may be held strictly liable for injuries caused by a defective product if the product was unreasonably dangerous and the defect existed at the time the product left the defendant’s control. This is a separate cause of action from negligence and is based on the concept of strict liability, which holds that a manufacturer is responsible for the safety of its products regardless of the level of care exercised in their production.  

B. **Defective Nature of the Product**  
The “EcoClean Pro” multi-surface cleaner was defective in its design and labeling. The product was not reasonably safe for its intended use, as it was not properly labeled with warnings or instructions regarding the necessity of dilution. The product was not designed to be used in an undiluted form, and its use in such a manner posed an unreasonable risk of harm to consumers.  

C. **Defect at the Time of Sale**  
The defect in the product existed at the time the product left the Defendant’s control. The Defendant failed to provide adequate warnings, instructions, and safety measures that would have made the product reasonably safe for its intended use. The defect was not the result of any misuse or alteration by the Plaintiff, but rather the result of the Defendant’s failure to ensure the product was safe and properly labeled.  

D. **Causation**  
The defective nature of the product was a direct and proximate cause of the Plaintiff’s injuries. The Plaintiff’s injuries were a foreseeable result of using the product in its undiluted form, which was not reasonably safe for its intended use. The Defendant’s failure to warn and to provide adequate instructions was a significant contributing factor to the Plaintiff’s harm.  

E. **Damages**  
The Plaintiff has suffered substantial damages as a result of the defective product, including medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and loss of enjoyment of life. These damages are directly attributable to the Defendant’s failure to ensure the product was safe and properly labeled.  

**VII. ALLEGATIONS AGAINST THE DEFENDANT**

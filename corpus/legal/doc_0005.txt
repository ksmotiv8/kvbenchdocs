**PRODUCT LIABILITY COMPLAINT**  
**Case No.: 24-789123**  
**Caption: Sarah Mitchell v. EcoSafe Manufacturing, Inc.**  

**Court: Superior Court of the State of California, County of Los Angeles**  
**Filed: April 5, 2024**

**I. INTRODUCTION**

This Complaint is brought by the Plaintiff, Sarah Mitchell, against the Defendant, EcoSafe Manufacturing, Inc., a corporation duly organized and existing under the laws of the State of California, and doing business in the State of California and elsewhere, for injuries sustained by the Plaintiff as a result of the Defendant’s alleged negligent and strictly liable conduct in the design, manufacture, and sale of a product known as the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System, model number ES-5000. The Plaintiff hereby alleges that the Defendant’s conduct was reckless, grossly negligent, and in violation of applicable product liability laws and common law principles. This Complaint sets forth the facts, legal standards, and legal theories that entitle the Plaintiff to relief against the Defendant for damages, including, but not limited to, economic losses, medical expenses, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and other consequential damages.

**II. JURISDICTION AND VENUE**

The Court of this jurisdiction has original jurisdiction over this action pursuant to California Code of Civil Procedure § 410.10, which provides that the Superior Court of the State of California has jurisdiction over actions involving claims of injury or damage caused by defective products. The venue of this action is proper in the Superior Court of the State of California, County of Los Angeles, as the Plaintiff resides within this county, and the Defendant, EcoSafe Manufacturing, Inc., has a place of business within this county. Additionally, the Defendant has conducted extensive business in this jurisdiction, including marketing, selling, and distributing the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System throughout the State of California. The actions giving rise to this litigation occurred in the County of Los Angeles, and the injuries sustained by the Plaintiff were directly related to the use of the product in this jurisdiction. Thus, this Court has both subject matter and personal jurisdiction over the parties and the claims asserted herein.

**III. PARTIES**

**A. Plaintiff**

The Plaintiff, Sarah Mitchell, is a natural person residing at 1234 Main Street, Los Angeles, California, 90012. She is the sole legal owner of a property located at 1567 Oak Avenue, Los Angeles, California, 90023, where the subject product was used. The Plaintiff is a competent adult with the legal capacity to bring this action and is not a minor, a corporate entity, or a foreign national. The Plaintiff has suffered substantial and permanent injuries, including but not limited to, chronic respiratory distress, neurological impairments, and physical pain, as a result of the use of the defective EcoSafe™ Water Filter System.

**B. Defendant**

The Defendant, EcoSafe Manufacturing, Inc., is a California corporation organized under the California Corporations Code, and is engaged in the business of manufacturing, distributing, and selling water filtration systems. The Defendant has its principal place of business at 4567 Innovation Drive, Santa Monica, California, 90401. The Defendant has been engaged in the sale and distribution of the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System, model number ES-5000, for several years, and has marketed and sold the product to consumers throughout the United States, with a significant presence in California. The Defendant has not disclosed any warnings, precautions, or disclaimers regarding the potential hazards or defects associated with the use of the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System.

**IV. FACTUAL BACKGROUND**

On or about May 3, 2023, the Plaintiff, Sarah Mitchell, purchased the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System, model number ES-5000, from a retail outlet located at 8901 Beverly Boulevard, Los Angeles, California. The product was advertised as a high-efficiency, eco-friendly water filtration system designed to remove contaminants and impurities from tap water. The product was marketed as safe for daily household use, including for drinking, cooking, and personal hygiene.

The Plaintiff installed the system in her home on May 5, 2023, and began using it for personal consumption on a regular basis. On June 12, 2023, the Plaintiff experienced an unexplained episode of severe respiratory distress and dizziness. She was taken to the emergency room at St. John’s Medical Center in Los Angeles, where she was diagnosed with acute respiratory failure and mild neurological impairment. The medical professionals at the hospital conducted a series of tests and found that the Plaintiff had been exposed to a toxic substance that was not listed in the product’s warning labels or safety information.

Following further investigation, the Plaintiff’s personal physician, Dr. James Wren, who is a board-certified internist and has been practicing in Los Angeles for over twenty years, conducted a thorough medical evaluation and determined that the toxic exposure was likely the result of the use of the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System. The physician testified that the product was designed and manufactured with materials that were not suitable for prolonged exposure to water, and that the filtration system was capable of leaching harmful chemicals into the water supply. Dr. Wren further opined that the design of the product was inherently defective and that the manufacturer failed to implement adequate safety measures to prevent such harm.

The Plaintiff continued to use the product for a period of time following the initial incident, during which she experienced recurring symptoms, including chest pains, nausea, and fatigue. On August 1, 2023, the Plaintiff was admitted to the hospital for a second time and was diagnosed with chronic respiratory conditions and mild cognitive dysfunction. The medical records, expert testimony, and laboratory findings all confirm that the Plaintiff was exposed to a toxic substance that originated from the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System. 

**V. CLAIMS FOR RELIEF**

**A. NEGLIGENCE**

**1. The Defendant’s Duty of Care**

The Defendant, as a manufacturer and seller of a consumer product, owed a duty of care to all users of the product, including the Plaintiff. Under California law, manufacturers and sellers are required to exercise reasonable care in the design, manufacture, and sale of their products to ensure that they are safe for their intended use. The Defendant owed a duty to the Plaintiff to provide a product that was free from defects that could cause injury or harm.

**2. The Defendant’s Breach of Duty**

The Defendant breached its duty of care by failing to conduct adequate testing of the EcoSafe™ Water Filter System to determine whether the product was safe for its intended use. The Defendant also failed to provide adequate warnings or instructions regarding the potential dangers associated with the product. The failure to provide such warnings was a direct cause of the Plaintiff’s injuries. The Defendant was aware, or should have been aware, that the product contained materials that could leach harmful substances into the water supply, but it did not take any steps to correct or warn users of this defect.

**3. Causation and Proximate Cause**

The breach of duty by the Defendant was a proximate cause of the Plaintiff’s injuries. The toxic exposure the Plaintiff suffered was a direct and foreseeable result of the Defendant’s failure to ensure the product’s safety. The Defendant’s negligence was a substantial factor in causing the Plaintiff’s injuries and the resulting damages.

**4. Damages**

As a direct and proximate result of the Defendant’s negligence, the Plaintiff has suffered significant physical, emotional, and financial damages. These include, but are not limited to, medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and diminished quality of life. The exact amount of damages remains to be determined at trial, but the Plaintiff is entitled to full and fair compensation for all losses suffered.

**B. STRICT LIABILITY – DESIGN DEFECT**

**1. The Product Was Defective in Design**

The EcoSafe™ Water Filter System, model number ES-5000, was defective in design. The product was not safe for its intended use, and the risk of harm outweighed any benefits the product might provide. The design of the product allowed harmful substances to leach into the water supply, and this risk was foreseeable to the manufacturer. The Defendant had a duty to design the product in a manner that would prevent such harm.

**2. The Defect Was Inherent in the Product**

The design defect was inherent in the product and not the result of any manufacturing error. The use of certain materials in the construction of the filter system was not suitable for the intended use, and this defect existed at the time the product was manufactured and sold. The Defendant was aware of the dangers associated with the use of these materials and failed to correct the design flaw.

**3. The Defect Was a Substantial Factor in Causing Injury**

The design defect was a substantial factor in causing the Plaintiff’s injuries. The use of the product, as intended by the manufacturer, led to the exposure of the Plaintiff to harmful substances. The Defendant’s failure to correct the design defect was a proximate cause of the Plaintiff’s injuries.

**4. The Product Was Sold in the Stream of Commerce**

The EcoSafe™ Water Filter System was sold in the stream of commerce to the Plaintiff. The Defendant had no control over the Plaintiff’s use of the product, and the product was not intended for commercial use. The Plaintiff was a consumer who used the product for its intended purpose, which was to provide safe and clean drinking water.

**5. Damages**

As a result of the Defendant’s failure to design the product safely, the Plaintiff has suffered significant physical, emotional, and financial damages. These include, but are not limited to, medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and diminished quality of life. The exact amount of damages remains to be determined at trial, but the Plaintiff is entitled to full and fair compensation for all losses suffered.

**D. STRICT LIABILITY – WARNING DEFECT**

**1. The Product Was Defective in Warning**

The EcoSafe™ Water Filter System, model number ES-5000, was defective in warning. The product failed to provide adequate warnings or instructions regarding the potential risks associated with its use. The Plaintiff was not informed of the dangers that could result from the use of the product, and this lack of information contributed to the injury sustained by the Plaintiff.

**2. The Defendant Had a Duty to Warn**

The Defendant owed a duty to provide adequate warnings and instructions regarding the potential risks associated with the use of the product. This duty includes the obligation to inform users of the risks of exposure to harmful substances, as well as the potential consequences of using the product in certain conditions. The Defendant failed to fulfill this duty.

**3. The Defendant Failed to Provide Adequate Warnings**


The Defendant failed to provide adequate warnings or instructions regarding the potential risks associated with the use of the product. The product did not contain any warnings, cautions, or disclaimers regarding the presence of harmful substances or the risks of exposure. The lack of warnings was a direct cause of the Plaintiff’s injuries.

**4. The Defendant Should Have Known of the Risks**

The Defendant should have known of the risks associated with the use of the product. The Defendant was aware, or should have been aware, that the product contained materials that could leach harmful substances into the water supply. The Defendant failed to correct or warn users of this risk.

**5. The Defect Was a Substantial Factor in Causing Injury**

The failure to provide adequate warnings was a substantial factor in causing the Plaintiff’s injuries. The Plaintiff was not informed of the risks associated with the use of the product, and this lack of information contributed to the injury sustained by the Plaintiff. The Defendant’s failure to provide adequate warnings was a proximate cause of the Plaintiff’s injuries.

**6. Damages**

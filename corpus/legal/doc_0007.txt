### **II. LEGISLATIVE HISTORY AND CONTEXT OF SECTION 112(B) OF THE CODE**  

Section 112(b) of the Federal Tax Code, as amended by the 2019 Tax Reform Act, establishes a comprehensive framework for determining the classification of individuals as independent contractors or employees for the purposes of income tax withholding and reporting. The original version of Section 112(b), enacted in 1977, was modeled on the common law test for independent contractors, which focuses on the degree of control exerted by the employer over the means and methods of performance of the work. The provision, however, was not merely a mechanical codification of the common law; it was also intended to serve as a regulatory tool to ensure that self-employed individuals were not unfairly burdened by the tax obligations imposed on employees.

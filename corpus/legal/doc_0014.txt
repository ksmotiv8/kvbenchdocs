**PREAMBLE**  
This document is issued by GlobalTech Solutions Inc. (hereinafter referred to as “GlobalTech” or the “Company”) to establish a comprehensive framework for the internal reporting of unethical, illegal, or otherwise inappropriate practices within the organization. The purpose of this policy is to ensure transparency, accountability, and ethical conduct throughout all levels of the organization, while simultaneously providing robust protections for individuals who report such activities in good faith. This policy is designed to align with the Company’s core values, which include integrity, accountability, and the promotion of a safe and ethical workplace.

In the course of their employment, all employees, contractors, interns, and any other individuals who are engaged by GlobalTech in any capacity (collectively referred to as “Employees”) may encounter situations involving potential violations of the Company’s code of conduct, applicable laws, or ethical standards. These situations may include, but are not limited to, financial fraud, corruption, discrimination, harassment, unsafe working conditions, environmental violations, and breaches of data privacy. The Company recognizes the importance of addressing such issues promptly and effectively, and thus commits to providing a secure and confidential mechanism through which such concerns may be reported and investigated.

This policy is intended to serve as both a guide and a binding obligation for all Employees. It outlines the procedures for reporting, the protections afforded to whistleblowers, the consequences for retaliation, and the expectations for all parties involved in the reporting and investigation process. By adopting this policy, GlobalTech reaffirms its commitment to upholding the highest standards of ethical behavior and accountability within its organization.

**ARTICLE I – DEFINITIONS AND SCOPE**  

**Section 1.1 – Definitions**  
For the purposes of this policy, the following terms shall have the meanings assigned below:  

(a) **Whistleblower** – Any current or former Employee, contractor, intern, or other individual who has reported or disclosed to GlobalTech, or to any designated reporting authority within the Company, any activity that the whistleblower reasonably believes to be a violation of the Company’s code of conduct, applicable laws, or ethical standards.  

(b) **Reporting Channel** – The formal, confidential, and secure method or platform designated by GlobalTech for the receipt of whistleblower reports, including but not limited to the internal reporting portal, direct communication with the Ethics Compliance Officer, or submission to the designated legal or human resources department.  

(c) **Protected Activity** – Any disclosure, report, or communication made by a whistleblower in good faith, which relates to a violation of the Company’s code of conduct, applicable laws, or ethical standards, and which is made in a manner that is consistent with the Company’s internal reporting procedures.  

(d) **Retaliation** – Any adverse action taken against a whistleblower by the Company, an employee, or any other individual, including but not limited to demotion, harassment, termination, or any other form of discrimination, in response to the whistleblower’s protected activity.  

(e) **Protected Individual** – A person who is, or has been, a whistleblower, or who is associated with a whistleblower in any manner, including but not limited to family members, colleagues, or any other individual who may be impacted by the whistleblower’s disclosure.  

(f) **Investigating Authority** – The designated department or individual within GlobalTech responsible for conducting investigations into whistleblower reports, including but not limited to the Ethics Compliance Office, the Legal Department, or an independent third-party investigator, as appropriate.  

(g) **Code of Conduct** – The internal policy document issued by GlobalTech which outlines the standards of behavior expected of all Employees, including but not limited to ethical conduct, compliance with laws, and the prevention of misconduct.  

(h) **Confidentiality** – The obligation of all parties involved in the whistleblower reporting process to maintain the secrecy of the whistleblower’s identity, the nature of the report, and any related investigation, unless disclosure is required by law or is necessary to protect the public interest.  

(i) **Good Faith** – The requirement that a whistleblower must have a reasonable belief, based on the information available at the time of the disclosure, that the activity in question constitutes a violation of the Company’s code of conduct, applicable laws, or ethical standards.  

(j) **Non-Retaliation** – The obligation of GlobalTech and all Employees to refrain from any form of retaliation against whistleblowers for engaging in protected activity, and to ensure that whistleblowers are not subject to any adverse employment action as a result of their disclosures.  

(k) **Internal Reporting Procedure** – The structured process through which whistleblower reports are received, reviewed, and acted upon, including but not limited to the submission of the report, the initiation of an investigation, and the determination of whether any violation has occurred.  

(l) **Protected Reporting** – The act of reporting a potential violation of the Company’s code of conduct, applicable laws, or ethical standards in a manner that is consistent with the Company’s internal reporting procedures, and that does not involve the disclosure of confidential information or privileged communications.  

(m) **Retaliation Prevention Measures** – The measures implemented by GlobalTech to ensure that whistleblowers are protected from retaliation, including but not limited to the establishment of clear internal reporting mechanisms, the appointment of a designated whistleblower protection officer, and the enforcement of non-retaliation policies.  

(n) **Compliance Officer** – The individual or department within GlobalTech responsible for overseeing the implementation of the whistleblower protection policy, ensuring that all reports are investigated thoroughly, and that all whistleblowers are afforded the protections outlined in this policy.  

(o) **Investigation** – The process of examining and evaluating a whistleblower report to determine whether a violation of the Company’s code of conduct, applicable laws, or ethical standards has occurred, and to assess the credibility and validity of the report.  

(p) **Confidential Reporting** – The requirement that all whistleblower reports be submitted in a manner that ensures the anonymity and confidentiality of the whistleblower, unless the whistleblower chooses to disclose their identity.  

(q) **Internal Policy** – Any policy, guideline, or standard issued by GlobalTech that governs the conduct of Employees, including but not limited to the Code of Conduct, the Anti-Discrimination Policy, the Data Privacy Policy, and the Corporate Compliance Policy.  

(r) **Applicable Laws** – The laws and regulations that apply to the operations of GlobalTech, including but not limited to labor laws, anti-corruption statutes, environmental regulations, data protection laws, and other relevant legal frameworks.  

(s) **Ethical Standards** – The principles and values that guide the conduct of GlobalTech and its Employees, including but not limited to integrity, honesty, transparency, accountability, and the promotion of a safe and ethical work environment.  

(t) **Confidential Information** – Any information that is not intended for public disclosure, including but not limited to trade secrets, confidential business strategies, financial data, and other proprietary information owned or controlled by GlobalTech.  

(u) **Privileged Communications** – Communications that are protected by attorney-client privilege, work product doctrine, or other legal protections, and which may not be disclosed without the consent of the party holding the privilege or as required by law.  

(v) **Designated Reporting Authority** – The specific person or department within GlobalTech to which whistleblower reports must be submitted, as outlined in this policy, including but not limited to the Ethics Compliance Officer, the Legal Department, or the Human Resources Department.  

(w) **Protected Disclosure** – Any disclosure of information made by a whistleblower that is not intended to be disclosed to the public, and that is made in the course of the whistleblower’s duties or responsibilities, and that relates to a violation of the Company’s code of conduct, applicable laws, or ethical standards.  

(x) **Whistleblower Reporting Portal** – The online platform or digital system provided by GlobalTech for the submission of whistleblower reports, which includes features such as secure login, encrypted communication, and anonymous reporting options.

(ab) **Investigation Duration** – The timeframe within which the investigation must be completed, which may vary depending on the complexity of the report and the nature of the allegations, but shall not exceed a reasonable period of time as determined by the Investigation Officer.

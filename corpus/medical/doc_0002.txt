### **14. Clinical Course and Management**

The patient was admitted to the ED after presenting with acute abdominal pain, vomiting, and diarrhea. His vital signs were within normal range except for tachycardia (102 bpm), hypotension (94/62 mmHg), and decreased urine output. He was assessed for dehydration, and a physical examination revealed mild epigastric tenderness and decreased bowel sounds.  

Initial laboratory studies showed mild elevation in WBC and slightly abnormal renal function (BUN slightly elevated). However, no signs of sepsis or acute organ failure were noted. The patient was diagnosed with **acute gastroenteritis with dehydration**.  

  In response, the patient was started on **IV fluids** (Normal Saline 500 mL bolus) and **oral rehydration therapy**. He was also monitored for electrolyte imbalances, and a repeat electrolyte panel was ordered to assess fluid status.  

Over the next 6 hours, the patient showed improvement in hydration status. His urine output increased, and his vital signs stabilized. His abdominal pain decreased, and he was able to tolerate oral intake.  

The patient was given **oral rehydration solution (ORS)** and instructed to increase fluid intake. He was also started on **loperamide 2 mg PO every 4 hours as needed** for diarrhea control.  

He was also advised to avoid dairy products and spicy foods and to monitor for signs of worsening symptoms. He was discharged from the ED after 6 hours of observation with a follow-up plan and outpatient care.

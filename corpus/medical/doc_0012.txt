**Specialty Referral Reason:**  

The patient presents with symptoms and laboratory findings consistent with **hypothyroidism** and **early menopause**. Given her history of thyroidectomy and current thyroid function tests, she may be experiencing **secondary hypothyroidism** or **hypopituitarism**. The elevated TSH, low Free T4, and high FSH and LH levels suggest **primary hypogonadism**, likely due to **premature ovarian insufficiency (POI)**. She has no history of prior ovarian surgery or cancer, but the combination of menopausal symptoms, irregular menstrual cycles, and elevated gonadotropins supports this diagnosis. Given her age (52 years), the possibility of **ovarian failure** is significant and warrants further evaluation.

**Clinical Concerns:**  
- **Secondary Hypothyroidism:** Given her history of thyroidectomy, she may be experiencing **hypopituitarism**, where the pituitary gland is not adequately producing thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), leading to a secondary form of hypothyroidism.  
- **Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI):** Her elevated FSH and LH, along with menstrual irregularities, suggest **POI**. This is a condition where the ovaries stop functioning normally before the age of 40, leading to symptoms similar to menopause.  
- **Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS):** Although her FSH and LH are elevated, her androgen levels are within normal range, making PCOS less likely.  
- **Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM):** Her fasting glucose is elevated, and she has a long-standing history of T2DM. She is currently managed with metformin and glimepiride.  
- **Obesity and Metabolic Syndrome:** Her BMI is 28.6, and she has an increased waist circumference, suggesting **obesity** and **metabolic syndrome**.  
- **Depression and Anxiety:** Her current mental health status is stable, but she has a history of mild depression and anxiety, managed with sertraline.

**Plan for Referral:**  
This referral is being made for **comprehensive endocrinologic evaluation** to determine the underlying cause of her symptoms, particularly **hypothyroidism** and **premature ovarian insufficiency (POI)**. The following investigations are requested:  
- **Thyroid Function Tests:**  
  - TSH  
  - Free T4  
  - Free T3  
  - Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO) Antibodies  
  - Thyroglobulin Antibodies (TgAb)  
- **Reproductive Hormone Panel:**  
  - AMH (Anti-Müllerian Hormone)  
  - Estradiol  
  - Prolactin  
  - Testosterone (for female)  
  - SHBG (Sex Hormone Binding Globulin)  
- **Ovarian Ultrasound:**  
  - To assess for **polycystic ovaries** or other structural abnormalities  
- **Bone Mineral Density (BMD) Scan:**  
  - Due to risk of **osteoporosis** in women with POI  
- **Fasting Insulin and Glucose Test:**  
  - To assess for **insulin resistance** and **metabolic syndrome**  
- **Cortisol Levels at 8 AM and 4 PM:**  
  - To assess for **hypopituitarism** or **adrenal insufficiency**  
- **Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) Evaluation:**  
  - If POI is confirmed, evaluate for **estrogen replacement therapy** and **progesterone** if appropriate  

- **Thyroid Hormone Replacement Therapy:**  
  - If secondary hypothyroidism is confirmed, initiate **levothyroxine** and titrate to therapeutic TSH levels
